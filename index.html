DTLab UTFORSK Partnership Program

The aim of this project is to develop novel DTLab-based methods for BLS education outside the classroom and for aquaculture education across geographic locations.

DTLab UTFORSK partnership program was launched in order to strengthen international collaboration between Norwegian institutions and partners in China, Brazil and the USA.

Project Overview

The Digital Twin Lab (DTLab) for International Biomedical Laboratory Science (BLS) Education project is a four-year project funded by the Norwegian Directorate for Higher Education and Skills. The project aims to enhance the quality of higher education in Norway, China and Brazil by developing and implementing a digital twin lab (DTLab) in lab courses, increasing student engagement in aquaculture and biomedical subjects, and strengthening the connection between theory and practice.

The project is hosted by the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) and partnered with Nankai University (Tianjin, China), Federal University of Paran√° (Paran√°, Brazil), and the University of New Hampshire (Durham, USA).

Highlights

  • Promote mobility of students and university staff to expand networks and enhance study quality.
  • Establish a common DTLab-based learning platform as a sustainable model for BLS education.
  • Increase students' motivation and competence through designing, developing, using, and evaluating DTLab systems across multiple learning programs, including clinical lab practice.
  • Integrate education, research, and innovation by developing DTLab-based learning approaches.
  • Strengthen links with future careers by connecting biomedical laboratory science (BLS) education with international clinical practice.
  • Follow-up and disseminate research results through publications, conferences, and seminars on DTLab‚Äôs educational impact.
