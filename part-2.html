Hemostasis Training Simulation (Unity Play / WebGL)

The webpage presents an online Unity-based experimental simulation platform hosted on Unity Play, titled Simulation Learning Lab. It is a browser-accessible interactive environment built with the Unity engine and deployed using WebGL technology, allowing users to run a 3D simulation directly without installing any local software. The platform is designed for simulation-driven learning, where users can interact with virtual scenes, objects, and procedural workflows that emulate real-world laboratory or training scenarios. Through mouse and keyboard interactions, users can explore the environment, follow predefined operational steps, and observe system feedback in real time, making it suitable for education, training demonstrations, and experimental prototyping. As a typical Unity Play deployment, the simulation emphasizes accessibility, visual realism, and interactivity, and is particularly well-suited for use as a supplementary teaching tool or as a proof-of-concept for virtual labs and simulation-based learning systems. Hemostasis refers to the processes by which the body causes bleeding from wounds to stop. The process depends on a cascade of interactions between different factors in the blood. Imbalances between these factors can affect the body's ability to maintain hemostasis. Common imbalances between factors can be detected and characterized by laboratoryexperiments performed on samples of patient plasma, including: Prothrombin Time-International Normalized Ratio (PT-INR) Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (APTT) Fibrinogen These experiments can be performed manually. In clinical settings the tests are typicallyconducted using automated equipment. In this course, you will simulate performing the experiments with partial automation using the Stago Start 4 Hemostasis Analyzer machine. The procedures for each experiment are similarbut differ in important was including the choice of reagent.
