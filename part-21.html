Detection of IgM Antibodies against *Treponema Pallidum* using ELISA

The purpose of this study is to use a commercial ELISA kit to analyze IgM antibodies against Treponema Pallidum in serum or plasma. ELISA is a plate-based assay technique designed for detecting and quantifying soluble substances such as peptides, proteins, antibodies, and hormones.

Methodology of ELISA

ELISA is based on a highly specific antibody-antigen interaction. The process involves several key steps:

  1. Coating/Capture ‚Äì Direct or indirect immobilization of antigens to the surface of microplate wells.
  2. Plate Blocking ‚Äì Addition of irrelevant protein to cover all unsaturated surface-binding sites.
  3. Probing/Detection ‚Äì Incubation with antigen-specific antibodies.
  4. Signal Measurement ‚Äì Detection of the signal generated via the direct or secondary tag on the specific antibody.
